Homogeneous coordinates (齐次坐标)

where $w$ defines a scale: the units of measure used by the coordinates.

Homogeneous Transformations Matrix (齐次变换矩阵)

where $R$ is used to do the rotation and $d$ is used to do the displacement.

Affine transforms （仿射变换）

The affine transforms are usually grouped in four classes:

  1. Translation

  2. Scaling
    Modifies the size, while maintaining constant its position and its orientation.
    Effects:

  • Enlarge & Shrink(缩小) & Deform(变形)

Can we achieve this by just adjusting the “w” value? Yes,but it will have some problems about subsequent transformations such as doing perspective transformation

  • Mirror

    Mirroring can be obtained by using negative scaling factors.

    Planar mirroring:  Assigning -1 to the scaling factor of the axis perpendicular(垂直) to the plane (x for plane yz: s_x=-1).

    Axial mirroring:   x and z for y-axis: $s_x=-1,s_z=-1$.

    Central mirroring:   $s_x=s_y=s_z=-1$.

  • Flatten(展平)

    A scaling factor of 0 in any direction, flattens the image along that axis. This however makes the transform matrix not invertible(不可逆), a feature that might require special handling.

  1. Rotation

    Varies the orientation of an object, leaving its position and size unchanged.

  • X-axis
  • Y-axis
  • Z-axis
  1. Shear (Take the y-axis as an example)

    As the value of y-axis increases, the object is linearly bent into a direction specified by a vector (in this case defined by two values: hx and hz ).

  • Y-axis
