3.2.2 Ideal formulations

Theorem (Meyer): Let $X\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n_1} \times\mathbb{Z}^{n_2}$ be the mixed integer feasible set (feasible region) of any MILP with rational coefficients, then conv(X) is a rational polyhedron. Moreover, all extreme points of conv(X) belong to X.
For bounded integer X, intuitive and no need for rational coefficients assumption.
Consequence:

If we knew conv(X) explicitly, we could solve the left-hand-side (M)ILP by solving a single Linear Program!

Clearly feasible region P of LP relaxation of any formulation satisfies $X\subseteq conv(X)\subseteq P$

Definition: Let $X\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n_1} \times\mathbb{Z}^{n_2}$ be any mixed integer feasible set, the ideal (perfect) formulation for X is the polyhedron $P\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n_1+n_2}$ with $P=conv(X)$.

Since ideal formulation is often of exponential size or dicult to determine, we strive for strong formulations.
