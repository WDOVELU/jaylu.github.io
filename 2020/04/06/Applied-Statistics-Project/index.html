II Some of my ideas (Combining feedback from the mentor)

We discussed, and here I wrote some analysis plans that we have not decided yet and would like to ask mentor’s opinion.

We consider that each country will show different growth trends when responding to the virus due to differences in population, economy, medical level, etc. And we temporarily consider dividing the analysis into two parts.

Part1: Analysis based on time series data and other fixed factors

We define: growth = the number of new cases per day, multiples = new cases in today/new cases in previous day.

Step1: Looking for the trends of various countries from the time series data.
Since only time series data is provided, I first observed the curve of the number of new cases per day and the multiple of the previous day in Italy.

We can find:

1. The histogram of daily new case can be divided into 3 stages: rapid growth period A, stable period B, and decline period C.

2. The red curve shows that the vibrations around 1 are gradually flattening( tendency ), and may show short-term periodicity (observe the 5 dashed boxes, but this is not certain)
Notice: Since most other countries are still in stage A, I cannot make more observations. For China, the division of the three stages is obvious, but the change of the multiple is more complicated.

Assuming the above observations are universal, perhaps we can obtain quantitative description of the curve in the following ways:

  • A. Based on histogram, set multiple threshold to divide into 3 stages and compute these variables for three stages respectively : the number of days, the number of cumulative cases,the average of daily new cases……..etc.

  • B. For the red curve:
    In time series analysis, we always divide a series($S$) into three parts: trend($T$), periodicity($P$), and noise($E$). And $S=T+P+E$.

    We found a special pca method for time series that can be used to extract trends and periodicities,which is called singular spectrum analysis(SSA). Here is wiki link and an example:

    Description and example(from page 30 in slides): https://www.slideshare.net/IsabelMagalhes13/some-contributions-to-pca-for-time-series
    Wiki: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singular_spectrum_analysis

Step2: Clustering the countries according to the quantitative description of the curve.

Step3: Analyze the growth performance of countries under different cluster;
Using tools such as correlation coefficients , ANOVA to find the factors that have a significant impact on growth from the supplementary data of population, national age distribution, hospital beds, economic level, etc;
Analyze the commonalities of the countries in a single group.

Step4: If everything goes well, maybe we can make some predictions using methods such as LR or time series analysis(ARIMA…).

Question: We are not sure whether this plan is effective and meaningful. So we want to ask the opinion of mentor,especially about SSA.

Part2: Consider building SIR models, but I don’t have much idea about it now.
